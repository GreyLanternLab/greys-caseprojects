## Overview

*The Digital Shadow* is a **fully fictional OSINT case study** developed for training and educational purposes.

The case simulates an online recruitment scenario designed to resemble patterns commonly associated with **misleading or exploitative recruitment practices**, including indicators that appear in documented human trafficking and fraud cases.

All entities, advertisements, domains and contact details used in this case are **synthetic**.

---

## Purpose of this Case

This case was created to:

- practice structured OSINT methodology  
- identify digital indicators and patterns  
- formulate and test hypotheses  
- apply ethical and legal boundaries in investigations  
- document analytical reasoning transparently  

It is **not** intended for accusation, enforcement or attribution.

---

## Scope & Methodology (High-Level)

The investigation follows a phased OSINT approach:

1. Context & scenario definition  
2. Scope and research questions  
3. Passive data collection (open sources only)  
4. Analytical pattern recognition  
5. Synthesis of findings  
6. Ethical reflection and limitations  

Detailed materials are organised per phase in the folders below.

---

## Folder Structure

| Folder | Description |

## Ethics & Disclaimer

- This case is **entirely fictional**  
- No real persons, organisations or victims are involved  
- No interaction, social engineering or deception was performed  
- All analysis is framed in terms of **indicators**, not accusations
